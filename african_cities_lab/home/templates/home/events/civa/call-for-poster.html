{% translate "Call for Poster & Demos" %}

{% blocktranslate %} The poster and demo session at the 1st African Cities Conference complements the research session and provides an opportunity to present recent findings and innovative implementations in an informal and interactive setting. During the poster and demo session, authors are encouraged to engage directly with conference participants and discuss their work. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} We invite authors to submit work related to African cities: digital and smart cities that address, but are not limited to, the conference's research themes (See ACC's topics). However, we also welcome papers that have already been seen in other venues but are to be shared with the ACC 2023 community. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} NB: Authors of research papers accepted for the Research track are explicitly invited to present their work through a poster or demo at the Poster and Demo session to further discuss their work with the community and expand their network with other researchers. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "Submission guidilines & Review process of Posters & Demos" %}

{% blocktranslate %} Authors should submit an abstract of no more than two pages (excluding references) for review. All submissions must be written in English, following the E3S web of conference formatting guidelines, and submitted electronically via the Easychair Submission system. {% endblocktranslate %}

  • {% blocktranslate %} Demos: Demo papers should present mature or prototype systems in which practical problems of African cities are addressed. Submissions should clearly define the objective, scope and audience. All submissions must provide a URL to an online version of their demo or, alternatively, provide a URL to a video showcasing the key features of their demo. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Posters: Posters submitted should describe recent research findings, ongoing research projects, or work in progress related to African cities. Additional links to relevant resources are encouraged to be included in the submission. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} Authors should indicate whether their submission is a poster or a demonstration article. Submissions should not be anonymized. Each submission will be reviewed by at least 2 members of the TPC via a single blind review process and the program chair will decide on final acceptance under recommendation of the Poster & Demos session chairs. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "Presentations and Publication of Posters & Demos" %}

{% blocktranslate %} The authors of accepted demos and posters will be published in the proceedings of the 1st African Cities Conference, in the E3S web of conference , in open access. All accepted papers will also be available on the conference website. Authors are encouraged to upload additional materials (e.g., images, videos) that will be available on the conference website. The authors of accepted demos and posters will be asked to provide a final version of their videos and posters two weeks before the conference. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} At least one of the authors must be a registered conference participant, and attend the poster/demo session to present the work. {% endblocktranslate %}
