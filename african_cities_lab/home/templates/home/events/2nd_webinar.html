{% translate "Overview" %}

{% blocktranslate %} By the year 2050, 35% world's population will be young and African, compared to the 15% in 2000. The accelerated urbanisation Africa has experienced last years came with the growth of the youngest part of its population, and it is bringing new issues. Nowadays, 60% of the African population is under 24 years of age. This specificity of the continent is an essential fact to include in urban planning. Considering the African youth as an important part of the development of different models of African cities, it is therefore legitimate positioning them as full actors and authors in the transformation of spaces and living environment. Those transformations will respond to the challenges the continent must face in the future such as sustainability, well-being, access to public utilities, education but above all, inclusion. During this "African Cities: What place for the youth?" webinar, experts and intervenants from all over the continent will answer the following questions: {% endblocktranslate %}
