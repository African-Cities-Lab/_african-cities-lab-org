{% translate "Overview" %}

{% blocktranslate %}By 2050, 35% of the world population will be young and African, compared to 15% in 2000.{% endblocktranslate %} {% blocktranslate %}The accelerated urbanisation that Africa has been experiencing in recent years goes along with an increase in the youngest part of its population, bringing new challenges to the cities.{% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %}Today, 60% of the African population is under the age of 24. This peculiarity of the continent is an essential fact to be taken into account in urban planning. Young people should have an important role in the development of African cities models. It is therefore legitimate to position them as actors and authors of the transformation of spaces and living environments that respond to the urban challenges facing the continent (sustainability, well-being, access to essential education services and inclusion.{% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %}In this webinar entitled "African cities: what place for young people?" experts will answer the questions of:

{% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% translate "Youth engagement through different movements;" %}
  • {% translate "Gender and equity in urban areas;" %}
  • {% translate "Urban youth activities." %}
