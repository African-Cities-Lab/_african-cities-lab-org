{% translate "The ACL Platform" %}

{% blocktranslate %} The African Cities Lab e-learning platform is available in French and English. This initiative funded by the State Secretariat for Economic Affairs SECO, allows all stakeholders involved in the construction, development and operation in urban areas to access quality education on sustainable urbanization to meet the 2050 challenges of African cities. {% endblocktranslate %}
{% translate "The MOOCs" %}

{% blocktranslate %} The MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) are online course, free of charge. Our MOOCs focus on all aspects of urban space planning and are divided into eight themes. They are taught by experts from Africa. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "Online training courses" %}

{% blocktranslate %} These are in-depth courses, designed for professionals involved in the construction of african cities of today and tomorrow who want to improve their professional practice and expertise. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "Hybrid training courses" %}

{% blocktranslate %} We offer deep-dive trainings to key decision makers. The courses are made of an eLearning component to gain foundation knowledge, as well as classroom training and field visits to deepen and contextualise knowledge. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "The International Conference of African Cities (ICAC)" %}

{% blocktranslate %} Challenging the current vision of African cities, digital cities are presented as a rational response to future demographic projections. African Cities Lab creates the "International Conference on African Cities (ICAC) - Digital Cities" , a major event that aims to increase the scope and develop synergies between research and entrepreneurship on the African continent. It is all about accelerating digitization of cities and promoting scientific excellence in Africa. The first edition of the Conference will take place in 2023 in Cotonou, Benin. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "African Cities Lab Webinars" %}

{% blocktranslate %} Through a series of 4 webinars, the partners of the African Cities Lab, in collaboration with EPFL, are providing insights for the political and scientific dialogue in the run-up to the Cotonou conference in 2023. The objective of these webinars is to discuss the major themes that make up the African digital city and set the framework for the agenda of the Conference. This webinar series will present research, trends, use cases and critical insights on how cities are or could be more effective in harnessing and leveraging digital technologies to promote sustainable development and achieve SDGs. {% endblocktranslate %}
