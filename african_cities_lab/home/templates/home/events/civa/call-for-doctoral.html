{% translate "Call for Doctoral Consortium & Spring School" %}

{% blocktranslate %} The ACC 2023 Doctoral & Spring School Consortium (DC) will take place virtually within the framework of the 1st International Conference on African Cities - Digital and Smart Cities, which will take place on May 23-24, 2023 at the Golden Tulip Hotel in Cotonou, Benin. This forum will allow early career doctoral students as well as post-graduate students to focus on the study of an issue related to African cities within the scope of the ACC 2023 conference themes. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} The DC offers the opportunity to present and discuss their doctoral research ideas and progress in a supportive, formative, yet critical environment, and to receive feedback from examiners, mentors, and peers in the field of their thesis topic. The doctoral consortium will also provide opportunities for networking and collaboration with other members of the ACC community. We welcome submissions in all areas of research related to African cities and particularly those of digital and smart cities (see "Topics of Interest"). {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} The event is open to both early career and late career doctoral candidates and asks them to formulate and submit a concrete research proposal, preferably supported by preliminary results. The proposal will be reviewed by the conference program committee. Accepted papers will be mentored by a designated mentor, usually an experienced researcher in the field. If accepted, students will be required to register and attend the event, which will include a series of interactive activities with a spring school. Submission details can be found below under "ACC's DC Submission Details". {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "ACC’s DC Submission Details " %}

{% blocktranslate %} Students must submit a 5-8 page (Not including references and appendices) description of their doctoral research proposal. Papers must be written in English and follow the E3S web of conference formatting guidelines available HERE. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} Articles must have a single author (the doctoral candidate). Supervisors, others involved and funders should be acknowledged in an Acknowledgements section. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} All proposals must be submitted electronically via the EasyChair conference submission system. https://easychair.org/conferences/?conf=civa2023 {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} All DC paper submissions must contain the following: {% endblocktranslate %}

  • {% blocktranslate %} Context and motivation: What is the social and research context of the research? {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Statement of the problem: What is the general problem that the research addresses? {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Related work: How does the research fit into the state of the art? {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Research Questions/Challenges: What questions does the research answer? {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Method/approach and evaluation: How are each of the research questions answered? How are the results evaluated? {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Preliminary results: (if available). {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Discussion and future work: What are the intermediate conclusions, what are the next steps planned? {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} The accepted CD papers will be published in the main proceedings of the 1st International Conference on African Cities and more precisely in the proceeding E3S web of conference indexed in Scopus, WoS and many others. Authors will have the opportunity to decline to be included in these proceedings. {% endblocktranslate %}
